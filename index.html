About Me

I am a master's student at Hiroshima University specializing in Special Education, with a concentration in fluency disorders. My research focuses on the lived experiences of people who stutter, inclusive education, disability studies, and qualitative research.

My current work explores the secondary school experiences and peer relationships of Chinese women who stutter, with particular attention to stigma, silence, identity, emotional suppression, and educational support systems.

Current Project

Peer Relationships of Chinese Women Who Stutter During Secondary School

This project adopts Interpretative Phenomenological Analysis to investigate how Chinese women who stutter experienced friendship, classroom participation, emotional suppression, peer misunderstanding, and the absence of school-based support.
